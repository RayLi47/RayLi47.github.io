About:

United Nations (UN) Numbers are four-digit numbers used world-wide in international commerce and transportation to identify hazardous chemicals or classes of hazardous materials. These numbers generally range between 0000 and 3500 and are ideally preceded by the letters "UN" (for example, "UN1005") to avoid confusion with other number codes.

For first responders in road accidents, knowing the substances carried in the shipments is vital. Located on placards and around the shipping containers, UN identification numbers are unique to individual materials or a group of similar materials. Using the UN number allows responders to identify more specific response guides than either the container shape or a placard without the ID number.

UN Number Scanner (UkNown Numbers) is a software developed for IOS, MacOS, Windows and Linux. The app scans for UN number placards and displays the corresponding contents in the cointainer.
